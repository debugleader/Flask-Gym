Our exercises section

Strength training can help you get stronger and look and feel better with just a few short sessions each week. You can do strength training with free weights such as barbells and dumbbells, weight machines, or with no equipment at all!

Bodyweight exercises

Not all bodyweight exercises and workouts are created equal though, and knowing that, we expect you’ll want to find out which moves are best, right?

Pushups

Traditional pushups are beneficial for building upper body strength. They work the triceps, pectoral muscles, and shoulders.

Squats

The most obvious benefit of squats is building your leg muscles – quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves. These drills also create an anabolic environment.

Pullups

The pullup is considered an advanced exercise. It’s more difficult than the chinup. But the pullup can be done on an assisted machine for beginners.

Freeweight exercises

Free weight exercises are some of the best ways to build strength as well as improve your functional fitness levels.

Curls

It’s a great movement for your upper body workout, whether you’re a beginner or an experienced lifter..

Shoulder press

Powerlifters, weightlifters, strongman competitors, and functional fitness athletes can all benefit from having a strong overhead press.

Deadlifts

Deadlifting increases your core strength and adds to core stability, as well as building overall muscle mass.
